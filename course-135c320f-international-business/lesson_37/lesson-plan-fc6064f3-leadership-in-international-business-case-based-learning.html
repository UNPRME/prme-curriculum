Learning Objectives

Apply leadership theories to real-world international business cases.
Understand various leadership styles and their relevance in international business.
Analyze the impact of leadership on global business strategies and organizational success.

PRME Characteristics

Students refine their understanding of leadership through continuous case analysis and group feedback.
Real-world case studies help students understand how leadership strategies affect business outcomes in diverse cultural settings.
Case-based learning makes leadership concepts more engaging and practical by relating them to global business scenarios.
Collaborative discussions and presentations encourage peer-to-peer learning and diverse viewpoints.
Students analyze leadership case studies and work collaboratively to propose solutions based on leadership theory.

SDG 8 Decent Work and Economic Growth - Exploring leaderships role in fostering growth through ethical management and inclusive practices in global businesses.

Required Materials

Case Study on Coca-Colas Leadership in Global Expansion; Articles on Leadership Theories (e.g., Transformational Leadership, Transactional Leadership, Servant Leadership); Whiteboard or flip chart for group discussions.

Lesson Activities

Hook / Intro: Define leadership and its significance in the context of international business; introduce leadership theories (Transformational, Transactional, Servant, Global Leadership). Reflection Question: How do leadership styles vary in different cultural settings, and why does this matter for international business?

1. Introduction to Leadership in International Business

Type: Introduction   Duration: 15 minutes

Define leadership and its significance in the context of international business. Introduce leadership theories: - Transformational Leadership Leaders who inspire and motivate employees by creating a shared vision. - Transactional Leadership Leaders who focus on structure, rewards, and punishments to manage employees. - Servant Leadership Leaders who prioritize the well-being and development of employees. - Global Leadership Understanding how leadership styles are adapted across different cultures. Reflection Question: How do leadership styles vary in different cultural settings, and why does this matter for international business?

2. Case Study Coca-Colas Leadership in Global Expansion

Type: Case Study   Duration: 30 minutes

Present a case study on Coca-Colas leadership strategies during its global expansion, particularly in emerging markets. Focus on the leadership style of Muhtar Kent and how his approach helped Coca-Cola navigate cultural and market challenges in Asia, Africa, and Latin America. In small groups, students will answer the following questions: 1. What leadership styles did Coca-Cola employ to expand into new global markets? 2. How did Coca-Colas leadership adapt to diverse cultural contexts? 3. What impact did leadership have on the companys global success? Formative Assessment: Evaluate the depth of analysis and understanding of leadership concepts as students work through the case study.

3. Group Activity Leadership Analysis and Strategy Proposal

Type: Group Activity   Duration: 30 minutes

In groups, students will select a multinational company (e.g., Unilever, Apple, Toyota) and analyze the leadership style(s) employed by the companys executives. Groups will then propose a leadership strategy for the company to expand into a new global market. The strategy should consider: - The leadership style best suited for the market. - How to manage cross-cultural teams. - How to overcome leadership challenges in diverse business environments. Each group will present their analysis and leadership strategy to the class. Formative Assessment: Evaluate the creativity, applicability, and cultural sensitivity of each groups leadership strategy.

4. Class Discussion The Role of Leadership in Organizational Culture

Type: Discussion   Duration: 15 minutes

Discuss how leadership shapes organizational culture in global businesses and the importance of aligning leadership with corporate values and mission. Explore the challenges leaders face when managing diverse teams and how to build an inclusive, high-performance culture across borders. Reflection Question: How can leaders ensure that their leadership style fosters innovation, inclusivity, and collaboration in a multicultural workforce?

Activity-specific Resources

Group Activity Leadership in International Business

Type: Group Activity

Description: Students will work in groups to develop a comprehensive leadership strategy for a multinational company expanding into a new international market by analyzing challenges and proposing strategies aligned with the companys mission, vision, and values.

Objective The objective of this group activity is to develop a comprehensive leadership strategy for a multinational company expanding into a new global market. Groups will analyze the challenges faced by the company in the international market and propose leadership strategies that align with the company's mission, vision, and values. Instructions 1. In your group, select a multinational company and choose a new international market for expansion. 2. Analyze the leadership challenges that the company will face in this new market, including cultural, economic, and regulatory differences. 3. Develop a leadership strategy that includes: - Leadership style(s) that will be most effective in the new market. - How to manage cross-cultural teams and leadership in diverse settings. - Ways to address the challenges the company will face in its expansion. 4. Present your findings to the class, including a detailed strategy for leadership in the new market. Reflection After the group activity, each team should submit a 1-2 page report summarizing their leadership strategy and how it addresses the challenges faced in the international market.

Assessment

Summative Assessment: Students will be assessed based on their participation in the case study analysis, their ability to apply leadership theories to real-world scenarios, and the quality of their group presentations on leadership strategies.

Data Analysis & Reflections

Review group presentations and class participation to assess students understanding of leadership theories and their application in international business contexts.

Was the case study on Coca-Colas leadership helpful in understanding the complexities of managing leadership in global business? How did the group activity allow students to apply leadership concepts in practical scenarios?

Differentiations

For Advanced Students: Encourage them to explore advanced leadership theories (e.g., Adaptive Leadership, Charismatic Leadership) and their application in multinational companies. For Struggling Students: Provide simplified case studies and additional support during group discussions to ensure understanding of leadership concepts.

Extensions

Homework: Research the leadership strategies of a company that has successfully expanded into multiple international markets. Write a report analyzing how leadership contributed to the companys success. Additional Reading: Leadership in a Globalized World An in-depth exploration of global leadership challenges and strategies.
